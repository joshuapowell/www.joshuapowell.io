Working with Data

An Adventurer’s Guide to Working with Data

I learned about using data to inform business early in my career. I had my real awakening while transitioning from a design studio to contracting for a startup.

The design studio was fast paced but relaxed. I made development decisions, but not without the management and design teams involvement. Huge marketing teams, car industry affiliates, and national non-profits provided data for the projects I worked on. They dropped data into my lap. Already cleaned up, already processed, and vetted with teams of lawyers. All I had to do was import it into a database.

When I arrived as a consultant at a 6 month old startup things weren't as cheery. The team was well credential. Most everyone was from a top 10 computer science, business, or design program. Outranked, I felt that I had no business working alongside this talented team. Yet, the data they worked with, was not on par with what I had gotten comfortable with at the design studio.

I had a long, uphill battle ahead of me and after 10 years, I have only passed the foothills.

In this series I will share tips on acquiring open data. I will introduce you to open-source tools for transforming your data. We will wrap up the series by helping you visualize your data.

I look forward to sharing with you. As you have questions along the way StackOverflow is priceless. If you have questions that aren't answered on StackOverflow or other online resources, please reach out.

Joshua Powell is a designer, developer, farmer, cyclist, surfer, camper, husband, father, and entrepreneur. He loves to help organizations with their data needs, practices regenerative agriculture, and can’t imagining doing anything else.
